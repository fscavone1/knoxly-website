About the project

Knoxly is a research project aiming to raise users' awareness concerning personal and private data they're about to disclose online, promoting privacy-oriented behaviors. Knoxly draws up from the experiences matured in the area of computational law and techno-regulation supporting rights protection in digital settings exploiting nudge-based solutions, i.e. gently influencing the users' behaviors to foster norm compliance.

Research scenario

With the enormous popularity of social media and other Web-related technologies, individuals are voluntarily disseminating during online activities a huge amount of both personal and sensitive information, that fundamentally represents an essential part of their private life. They are often unaware of the side effects of such practice and sometimes with the same unconsciousness they share private information of close friends or acquaintances. Therefore, there is an increasing need of instruments capable of helping them acknowledge the risks related to disclosure of private information, the exact moment this leakage happens and how to give them control on how manage and protect their data.

Knoxly in a nutshell

Knoxly is a prototype plugin for Google Chrome, which alerts/warns you if you are about to disclose private and / or personal information online. Knoxly exploits machine learning models and advanced natural language processing techniques to classify the type of content (topic) and the level of sensitivity of such content within text messages. Knoxly provides awareness, does not block the sending of a message, the final decision on whether or not to share the content it is up to you. Knoxly acts as a trusted entity, it does not use or store your personal data in any way other than to provide you with the services abovementioned. Additionally, Knoxly delivers online learning capabilities, adapting to your privacy attitudes by learning from your feedback. Knoxly currently runs on www.gmail.com and www.telegram.com, but will soon be available for www.twitter.com, www.reddit.com and other popular media.
